Banking · Youth Finance · PM Course Case Study (EntryLevel)

Kuda Teen Debit Card

Role: Product Manager (Concept & Planning) Type: Course Portfolio Project Market: Nigeria — Teens 11–18

Context

This is a product concept case study completed as part of my EntryLevel Product Management Level 1 certification. It demonstrates my ability to apply PM frameworks — from problem discovery through to MVP definition and prototype scoping — in a realistic product context.

The project was set within the Kuda Microfinance Bank ecosystem. All research, prioritization, and design decisions were conducted independently as part of the programme.

Problem Statement

Teenagers aged 11–18 in Nigeria need to make offline and online payments but cannot open bank accounts. This leaves them dependent on cash — which creates safety risks, zero parental spending visibility, and blocked access to online marketplaces and digital services.

Solution

A prepaid debit card product that lives inside the Kuda mobile app, requiring no bank account. Teens use the card for purchases; parents top up balances, set spending limits, receive real-time notifications, and can freeze the card instantly — all from the Kuda app.

Target Market

End Users: Teens aged 11–18 in urban/semi-urban Nigeria

Buyers: Parents aged 28–50 with Kuda accounts

Key Cities: Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt, Enugu, Ibadan, Delta

Market Signal: E-payment value grew ₦280T → ₦384T (2024–2025)

User Research

Conducted 3 user interviews to validate the problem space. Key validated pain points: cash loss and theft risk, inability to monitor teen spending, difficulty accessing online payments for school subscriptions and e-commerce, and desire for parental controls without full account responsibility.

Prototype

Produced a clickable Figma prototype of the "Teen Card" section within the Kuda app, scoped to the parent-facing management flow — including card setup, spending limits, transaction history, and freeze/unfreeze controls.

Moniepoint — Product Teardown

Type: Competitive Product Analysis Date: March 2026 Focus: UX, Distribution, Monetisation, Support

Overview

An in-depth product teardown of Moniepoint — Nigeria's leading merchant banking and POS payments platform. I evaluated the product across five dimensions, scoring each on a five-point scale, and provided structured recommendations for each gap identified.

What Moniepoint Gets Right

Onboarding Design — Phone number first, USSD OTP for low-connectivity areas, account in under 5 minutes. Built for its actual users.

Distribution as Strategy — POS agents across 33 states, Relationship Manager model, 48-hour terminal delivery.

Moniebook Data Flywheel — Payments generate transaction data → credit decisions → merchant growth → more transactions. Creates genuine switching costs.

Key Gaps & Recommendations

Customer Support — No in-app dispute tracker, no proactive notifications, no escalation SLA paths. Recommendation: real-time in-app dispute tracker with status updates.

Personal Banking Identity — ₦10 transfer fee vs free competitors, no cross-product rewards. Recommendation: tiered rewards linking merchant activity to personal account benefits.

Session Stability — App terminates sessions without warning during active transactions. Recommendation: proactive push notifications for any account restrictions or session changes.
